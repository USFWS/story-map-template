Chesapeake Bay Field Office
Poplar Island

The U.S. Army Corps of Engineers worked with the Service to restore natural island habitats in the Chesapeake Bay that were lost due to increases in armored shorelines. Dredge material from shipping channels and the Baltimore harbor were brought to the center of the Bay to re-create this major nesting area for waterfowl, shorebirds, and a host of endangered species.

Chesapeake Bay Nutria Eradication Program
Cambridge, MD

Marshes throughout the Delmarva peninsula were being lost at an alarming rate due to the explosion nutria (an aquatic rodent) population. These critters eat the roots of marsh vegetation causing complete thousands of acres of tidal wetlands. Congress appropriated funds to eradicate the species from the Chesapeake Bay watershed, which formed the basis of my first assignment as a GIS analyst.
